10  norm.cpp -- normally distibuted random numbers
11 
12  The following class allows you to create MTRand objects that are
13  independently seeded upon construction and return normally
14  distributed random numbers.
15 
16  This function generates normally distributed random numbers
17  with mean of mu and standard deviation of sigma, using the
18  polar method of Marsaglia and Bray, "A Convenient Method for
19  Generating Normal Variables", _SIAM Rev._, 6: 260-264 (1964).
20 
21  The algorithm is as follows:
22 
23  1. Generate two uniformly distributed numbers, U1 and U2.
24  Let Vi = 2*Ui-1 for i=1,2, and let W = V1*V1 + V2*V2.
25  2. If W > 1, go back to step 1. Otherwise, let
26  Y = sqrt(-2*ln(W)/W), X1 = V1*Y, and X2 = V2*Y. Then
27  X1 and X2 are normally distributed with mean of 0 and
28  variance of 1.
29  3. Random numbers with mean of mu and standard deviation of sigma
30  are calculated by: X_prime = mu + sigma X.
31 
32  Note that numbers are generated in pairs. On odd-numbered calls
33  to operator(), pairs are calculated. On even-numbered calls,
34  the second value is returned.
35 
36  Modified from norm.c, from xneuron3
